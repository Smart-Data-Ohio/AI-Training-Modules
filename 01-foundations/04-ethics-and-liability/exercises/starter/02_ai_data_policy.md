# Exercise 02: AI Acceptable Use Policy

## The Task

Draft a client-facing AI acceptable use policy for a consulting engagement. This is a document you'd include in a project kickoff or SOW addendum to set expectations about how AI tools will (and won't) be used on the project.

## Context

You're working on a project for a mid-size financial services company. They're not in a highly regulated space (no HIPAA), but they handle customer financial data and are SOC 2 compliant. They've asked your team to build a customer-facing dashboard with some AI-powered features (natural language search, automated report summaries).

## Self-Assessment

After completing the policy, review it against these criteria:

- [ ] Would a non-technical client stakeholder understand it?
- [ ] Does it cover the specific risks of the financial services context?
- [ ] Are the rules specific enough to follow? (Not just "be careful with data")
- [ ] Does it protect both Smart Data and the client?
- [ ] Is it short enough that people will actually read it?
- [ ] Does it address what happens when something goes wrong?
- [ ] Would you feel comfortable if this were exhibit A in a lawsuit?

## Stretch Goal

If you want to go further, write a one-page version of this policy as a "quick reference card" that developers can keep at their desk. The full policy is the legal document; the quick reference is the practical one.
