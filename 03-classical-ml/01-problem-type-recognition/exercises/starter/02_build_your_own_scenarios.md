# Exercise 02: Build Your Own Scenarios

## The Task

Now flip it around. Instead of classifying someone else's problems, write your own. Think about projects you've worked on, clients you've talked to, or business problems you've heard about — and frame them as ML problems.

**Time estimate:** ~30 minutes

## Instructions

Write 3 ML problem descriptions. For each one:

1. **Describe the scenario** in 2-3 sentences, written as if a client is explaining it to you
2. **Identify the problem type** (regression, classification, clustering, or anomaly detection)
3. **List the input features (X)** — what data would you need?
4. **Define the target (Y)** — what are you predicting or discovering?
5. **Explain why ML** — why can't this be solved with a simple rule, query, or lookup table?

**Requirements:**
- Include at least 2 different problem types
- Make them realistic — scenarios you could actually encounter in consulting
- Be specific about the features. "Customer data" is too vague. "Tenure, monthly charges, contract type, support ticket count" is specific.

## Self-Check

After writing your scenarios, review them against these criteria:

- [ ] Are the problem types correctly identified?
- [ ] Are the input features specific enough that someone could actually collect the data?
- [ ] Is the target clearly defined (a number for regression, a label for classification, etc.)?
- [ ] Could you explain the "why ML?" answer to a non-technical client?
- [ ] Did you include at least 2 different problem types?
